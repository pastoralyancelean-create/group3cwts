Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan

Feeding Program para sa Kabataang Katutubo

Community-based initiative · Conrazon, Bansud, Oriental Mindoro

Presented by: Khim Joethel Zaulda & NutriBo Team

Overview

The program "Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan: Feeding Program for Kabataang Katutubo" is a community-based initiative designed to address the issue of malnutrition among Indigenous Peoples (IP) children in Conrazon, a community located in Bansud, Oriental Mindoro.

Many children in indigenous communities face limited access to nutritious food and proper health support, making them vulnerable to malnutrition and its long-term effects on growth, learning, and overall well-being.

This program aims to provide nutritious and balanced meals to Indigenous children in order to improve their nutritional status and support their physical and cognitive development.

Overview — continued

Through regular feeding activities, the initiative seeks to help children maintain adequate nutrition, increase their energy levels, and promote better participation in school and community activities.

In addition to food distribution, the program will also incorporate nutrition education for parents and community members to raise awareness about proper child nutrition, healthy food preparation, and the importance of balanced diets.

By empowering families with knowledge and practical strategies, the program aims to promote sustainable health practices within the community.

Overview — final

Through collaboration with volunteers, community leaders, and local stakeholders, the program aspires to reduce the prevalence of malnutrition among Indigenous children and contribute to building a healthier, more active, and resilient community.

The initiative emphasizes inclusivity, cultural respect, and community participation to ensure that the program effectively responds to the needs of the Indigenous population it serves.

Goal

The goal of Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan: Feeding Program para sa Kabataang Katutubo is to help reduce malnutrition among Indigenous Peoples (IP) children by providing nutritious meals and promoting awareness about proper nutrition, contributing to healthier and more active children in the community.

Objectives

  • To provide nutritious meals to IP children who are experiencing or at risk of malnutrition.
  • To improve the health and energy levels of the children through regular and balanced food intake during the feeding activity.
  • To educate children and parents about the importance of proper nutrition and healthy eating habits.
  • To encourage community participation among students, teachers, and local leaders in addressing child malnutrition.
  • To promote compassion, volunteerism, and social responsibility among student volunteers through community service.

Project Evaluation

The project "Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan: Feeding Program para sa Kabataang Katutubo" will be evaluated based on how well it helps Indigenous Peoples (IP) children in Conrazon, Bansud, Oriental Mindoro.

The evaluation will focus on whether the program is able to provide nutritious food and raise awareness about proper nutrition among children and their parents.

The team NutriBo will observe the participation of the children during the feeding activity and check if the meals provided are helpful for their health and energy.

Project Evaluation — continued

Feedback from the children, parents, volunteers, and community leaders will also be collected to know their opinions and suggestions about the program.

The evaluation will also look at how well the activity was organized, including teamwork, coordination with the community, and support from donors and volunteers.

Any challenges that may happen during the program will be recorded so that the team can improve future activities.

Overall, the evaluation will help the team understand the impact of the feeding program and guide them in improving future programs for Indigenous children in the community.

Final Narrative Report / Self Analysis

As a team, we realized that community service is not just about distributing food—it is about building trust and relationships. The smiles on the children's faces and the tears of gratitude from some mothers reminded us why we chose this project.

Individually, we grew: Khim learned leadership under pressure, Eunice became more confident in financial management, and the rest of us discovered that even small contributions (washing dishes, playing with kids, or simply listening) can create lasting impact.

This project proved that when students, local government units, and private citizens work together, real change is possible even in remote indigenous communities.

Self Analysis — continued

The experience strengthened our belief that addressing malnutrition is not only a health issue but also a matter of dignity, equity, and love for the next generation.

We are proud of what we accomplished, but we also recognize that one day of feeding is only the beginning. Sustainable change requires continued partnership with the barangay, regular monitoring of the children's nutritional status, and more programs like this in the future.

The "Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan" feeding program may have ended, but the lessons, friendships, and sense of purpose it gave us will remain. We, the NutriBo team, commit to carry this spirit of service into our future endeavors and hope that other students will be inspired to do the same.
