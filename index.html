Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan

Feeding Program para sa Kabataang Katutubo

Conrazon, Bansud, Oriental Mindoro

Presented by: Khim Joethel Zaulda & NutriBo Team

Overview

A community-based initiative addressing malnutrition among Indigenous Peoples (IP) children in Conrazon, Bansud, Oriental Mindoro.

Many indigenous children face limited access to nutritious food and proper health support, making them vulnerable to malnutrition and its long-term effects on growth, learning, and well-being.

This program provides nutritious balanced meals to improve their nutritional status and support physical and cognitive development.

Program Components

Through regular feeding activities, we help children maintain adequate nutrition, increase energy levels, and promote better participation in school and community activities.

The program incorporates nutrition education for parents and community members to raise awareness about proper child nutrition, healthy food preparation, and balanced diets.

By empowering families with knowledge and practical strategies, we promote sustainable health practices within the community.

Community Partnership

Through collaboration with volunteers, community leaders, and local stakeholders, the program aspires to reduce malnutrition prevalence among Indigenous children.

The initiative emphasizes inclusivity, cultural respect, and community participation to ensure effective response to the needs of the Indigenous population.

Together, we're building a healthier, more active, and resilient community.

Goal

To reduce malnutrition among Indigenous Peoples (IP) children by providing nutritious meals and promoting awareness about proper nutrition.

Contributing to healthier and more active children in the community through sustainable feeding programs and education initiatives.

Objectives

  • Provide nutritious meals to IP children experiencing or at risk of malnutrition
  • Improve health and energy levels through regular balanced food intake
  • Educate children and parents about proper nutrition and healthy eating habits
  • Encourage community participation in addressing child malnutrition
  • Promote compassion, volunteerism, and social responsibility through service

Action Plan

  • Phase 1: Needs assessment and beneficiary identification
  • Phase 2: Secure donations and partnerships
  • Phase 3: Procure food supplies and organize logistics
  • Phase 4: Conduct feeding sessions and nutrition education
  • Phase 5: Collect feedback from beneficiaries
  • Phase 6: Evaluate program impact and document lessons

Project Evaluation

The project will be evaluated based on how well it helps IP children in Conrazon, focusing on whether the program provides nutritious food and raises awareness about proper nutrition.

Team NutriBo will observe children's participation during feeding activities and check if the meals are helpful for their health and energy.

Feedback from children, parents, volunteers, and community leaders will be collected to gather opinions and suggestions.

Evaluation Process

The evaluation will assess activity organization, including teamwork, community coordination, and support from donors and volunteers.

Any challenges during the program will be recorded so the team can improve future activities.

Overall, the evaluation will help understand the feeding program's impact and guide improvements for future programs serving Indigenous children.

Team Reflection

As a team, we realized that community service is not just about distributing food—it's about building trust and relationships.

The smiles on children's faces and tears of gratitude from mothers reminded us why we chose this project.

Individually, we grew: Khim learned leadership under pressure, Eunice became more confident in financial management, and the rest discovered that even small contributions create lasting impact.

This project proved that when students, LGUs, and citizens work together, real change is possible even in remote indigenous communities.

Our Commitment

The experience strengthened our belief that addressing malnutrition is not only a health issue but also a matter of dignity, equity, and love for the next generation.

We recognize that one day of feeding is only the beginning. Sustainable change requires continued partnership, regular monitoring, and more programs like this.

The "Busog na Bata, Masiglang Pamayanan" feeding program may have ended, but the lessons, friendships, and sense of purpose it gave us will remain.
